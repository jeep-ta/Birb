Bird Conservation

Protecting Our Feathered Friends for Future Generations

The Conservation Challenge

Birds face unprecedented threats from habitat loss, climate change, pollution, and human activities.

Nearly 1 in 8 bird species is threatened with extinction. We've lost 3 billion birds in North America since 1970, a 29% decline. The situation is urgent, but there's hope: conservation efforts can and do work when we act together.

Major Threats to Birds

Habitat Loss

Deforestation, urbanization, and agricultural expansion destroy critical breeding and feeding grounds.

Impact: Primary threat to 85% of endangered species
Climate Change

Rising temperatures alter migration patterns, food availability, and breeding cycles.

Impact: Affects timing of migration and food sources
Human Impact

Window collisions, domestic cats, pesticides, and light pollution cause billions of deaths annually.

Impact: 3 billion birds lost in North America since 1970

Conservation Success Stories

These inspiring examples show that conservation works when we commit to it.

Bald Eagle Recovery
Success: Endangered → Least Concern

Once down to just 417 breeding pairs in 1963 due to DDT pesticide poisoning, the Bald Eagle made a remarkable recovery after DDT was banned. Today, over 300,000 individuals thrive across North America.

Key Actions: Captive breeding, lead ammunition reduction, habitat restoration

California Condor
Progress: 27 → 500+ individuals

By 1987, only 27 California Condors remained. An intensive captive breeding program saved the species from extinction. Today, over 500 condors live in the wild and captivity combined.

Key Actions: Captive breeding, lead ammunition reduction, habitat restoration

How You Can Help

Everyone can contribute to bird conservation. Here's how to make a difference.

Bird-Friendly Spaces
  • Plant native trees and shrubs
  • Provide clean water sources
  • Install bird feeders safely
  • Create nesting opportunities
Reduce Hazards
  • Apply window decals
  • Keep cats indoors
  • Turn off lights at night
  • Avoid pesticides
Get Involved
  • Join local bird clubs
  • Participate in bird counts
  • Volunteer for habitat restoration
  • Support conservation groups
Advocate
  • Support conservation policies
  • Educate others
  • Make sustainable choices
  • Report bird crimes

Take Action Today

Your choices matter. Even small actions can have a significant impact when millions of people participate.
