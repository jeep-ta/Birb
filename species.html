Species Gallery

Discover the Incredible Diversity of Birds

Tropical Birds

Scarlet Macaw

Scientific Name: Ara macao

A large, colorful parrot native to Central and South America. Known for vibrant red, yellow, and blue plumage.

Conservation: Least Concern
Indian Peafowl

Scientific Name: Pavo cristatus

Famous for the male's spectacular iridescent tail feathers used in courtship displays.

Conservation: Least Concern
Greater Flamingo

Scientific Name: Phoenicopterus roseus

The most widespread flamingo species, known for pink plumage from carotenoid-rich diet.

Conservation: Least Concern

Birds of Prey

Bald Eagle

Scientific Name: Haliaeetus leucocephalus

The national bird of the United States, known for its white head and impressive wingspan up to 7.5 feet.

Conservation: Least Concern
Barn Owl

Scientific Name: Tyto alba

Recognized by its heart-shaped face. A silent nocturnal hunter found on every continent except Antarctica.

Conservation: Least Concern
Peregrine Falcon

Scientific Name: Falco peregrinus

The fastest animal on Earth, reaching speeds over 240 mph during hunting dives.

Conservation: Least Concern

Songbirds

American Robin

Scientific Name: Turdus migratorius

A common sight across North America, known for its orange breast and melodious song.

Conservation: Least Concern
Northern Cardinal

Scientific Name: Cardinalis cardinalis

Brilliant red males are easily recognized. Both sexes sing complex songs year-round.

Conservation: Least Concern
Blue Jay

Scientific Name: Cyanocitta cristata

Intelligent and adaptable, known for its loud calls and striking blue plumage with white and black markings.

Conservation: Least Concern

Endangered Species

Conservation Alert: These species require our immediate attention and protection efforts.
Spix's Macaw

Scientific Name: Cyanopsitta spixii

Extinct in the wild since 2000. Conservation breeding programs are working to reintroduce this species.

Conservation: Critically Endangered
Philippine Eagle

Scientific Name: Pithecophaga jefferyi

One of the world's largest and most powerful eagles. Only 400 pairs remain in the wild.

Conservation: Critically Endangered
Kākāpō

Scientific Name: Strigops habroptilus

A flightless nocturnal parrot from New Zealand. Intensive conservation has saved this unique species.

Conservation: Critically Endangered
